Expert Help with Bailiffs

Understanding your rights and options when dealing with bailiffs and enforcement agents.

What Can Bailiffs Take?

Understanding what bailiffs can and cannot take is crucial when facing enforcement action. Here's what you need to know:

Items bailiffs CAN take:

  • Luxury items like televisions, games consoles, and jewellery
  • Vehicles that are not essential for your work or daily life
  • Money, banknotes, and coins
  • Shares and bonds

Items bailiffs CANNOT take:

  • Essential household items like your cooker, washing machine, and fridge
  • Tools, books, or equipment you need for your job or study (up to a total value of £1,350)
  • Items that belong to someone else
  • Your phone or computer if you need it for work or study
  • Your pet

How Should You Interact with Bailiffs?

If a Bailiff Visits Your Home:

  • You don't have to open the door - you can speak through a window or letterbox
  • Don't let them in unless you understand your rights and the implications
  • If you do let them in, they can return and re-enter without permission
  • Ask to see their identification and enforcement documents
  • Check the debt is valid and the fees are correct
  • Never let them in if they haven't given proper notice

Safe Communication Tips:

  • Keep doors locked and windows closed
  • Use a chain on the door if you need to open it
  • Don't let them into your home unless you're certain of your rights
  • Ask for everything in writing
  • Don't agree to payment plans you can't afford

Bailiff Charges and Fees

Understanding the new fee structure introduced to make bailiff charges more straightforward:

Fee Type Amount When It Applies
Compliance Fee £75 When your case is transferred from the local authority to the bailiff
Enforcement Fee £235 + 7.5% of debts over £1,500 When bailiffs make their first visit to your premises
Sale Fee £110 + 7.5% of debts over £1,500 When bailiffs start removing or preparing items for sale

Important: Bailiffs must give seven days' notice before a visit, and can only visit between 6am and 9pm.

When Can Bailiffs Use Force?

Technically, bailiffs are not permitted to use force to enter your property. However, there are certain situations in which the "reasonable force" rule comes into play:

  • When they have obtained a court order
  • When you have previously granted them peaceful entry
  • For some specific debts like unpaid criminal fines or tax

This does NOT permit them to take extreme measures, such as pushing past you or breaking a window.

The Difference Between Bailiffs and Enforcement Agents

There is often confusion between bailiffs and enforcement agents, but they are essentially the same. In a recent review of bailiff regulations, they were renamed "enforcement agents" and subjected to a simplified set of rules. The changes not only encompass what they can or cannot do when visiting your property but also include new fees that may be applied at different stages of the process.
