Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your personal information

Introduction

Stop the Bailiff ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting and respecting your privacy. This policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your personal information when you use our website and services.

We comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Last updated: January 2024

Information We Collect

Personal Information You Provide:

  • Name and contact information (email, phone number, address)
  • Financial information (debt amounts, income, expenses)
  • Employment status and details
  • Information about your property and assets
  • Details about your debt situation and bailiff visits
  • Communications with us (emails, phone recordings, chat transcripts)

Information We Automatically Collect:

  • IP address and browser type
  • Pages you visit and how you interact with our site
  • Time and date of your visit
  • Referring website addresses
  • Cookies and similar technologies (see our Cookie Policy below)

How We Use Your Information

We use your personal information to:

  • Provide debt advice and assess your situation
  • Match you with appropriate debt solutions
  • Connect you with our trusted partners who can help
  • Respond to your enquiries and provide customer support
  • Improve our website and services
  • Comply with legal obligations
  • Prevent fraud and ensure security

Legal Basis for Processing

We process your information based on:

  • Consent: When you agree to us contacting you
  • Contract: To provide services you've requested
  • Legal obligation: To comply with laws and regulations
  • Legitimate interests: To improve our services and prevent fraud

Sharing Your Information

We may share your information with:

  • Trusted Partners: Licensed insolvency practitioners and debt solution providers who can help with your specific situation
  • Service Providers: IT services, hosting providers, and analytics services
  • Legal Authorities: When required by law or to protect our legal rights

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your information, including:

  • Encryption of data in transit and at rest
  • Secure servers and access controls
  • Regular security assessments and monitoring
  • Staff training on data protection

However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Your Rights

Under GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Access: Request a copy of your personal data
  • Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal obligations)
  • Restriction: Limit how we use your data
  • Portability: Receive your data in a structured format
  • Objection: Object to certain types of processing
  • Withdraw consent: At any time, where processing is based on consent

To exercise these rights, please contact us at privacy@stopthebailiff.co.uk

Data Retention

We retain your personal information only as long as necessary to provide services and comply with legal obligations. Typically, this means:

  • If you use our services: for 6 years after our last contact (to comply with financial regulations)
  • If you don't proceed with services: for 2 years after last contact
  • Website analytics data: 26 months

Cookies

Our website uses cookies to improve your experience. Cookies are small text files stored on your device. We use:

  • Essential cookies: Necessary for website function
  • Analytics cookies: To understand how visitors use our site
  • Preference cookies: To remember your settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect website functionality.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We'll notify you of any material changes by posting the new policy on this page with an updated revision date.
