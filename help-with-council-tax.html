Help with Council Tax Debt

Expert advice on dealing with council tax arrears and stopping bailiff action

Understanding Council Tax Debt

Council tax arrears can quickly escalate if not addressed. When you fall behind on payments, your local council can take enforcement action, which may include:

  • Issuing a summons and obtaining a liability order
  • Deducting payments directly from your wages or benefits
  • Passing the debt to enforcement agents (bailiffs)
  • In extreme cases, committal to prison (though very rare)

Options for Dealing with Council Tax Debt

✓ Arrange a Payment Plan

Contact your local council to arrange an affordable repayment plan. Most councils are willing to accept reasonable offers if you can demonstrate you can't pay the full amount.

✓ Check for Reductions

You may be eligible for:

  • Council Tax Support (if you're on a low income)
  • Single person discount (25% off)
  • Disability reductions
  • Exemptions for students or certain groups

✓ Challenge the Liability Order

If you believe the council tax bill is incorrect, you may be able to challenge it. Grounds for challenge include:

  • Incorrect banding
  • You're not the liable person
  • The property should be exempt
  • Procedural errors by the council

✓ Deal with Bailiffs

If bailiffs are already involved, you still have options. You can:

  • Make a payment arrangement with the bailiff
  • Apply to the court to suspend the liability order
  • Seek professional debt advice

Bailiff Fees for Council Tax

When council tax debt is passed to bailiffs, you'll be liable for their fees as well. The current fee structure is:

Stage Fee What Happens
Compliance Stage £75 Case passed to bailiff, notice sent
Enforcement Stage £235 (plus 7.5% of debt over £1,500) First visit to your property
Sale Stage £110 (plus 7.5% of debt over £1,500) Removal of goods for sale

Important: If you pay the full debt plus the compliance fee before the first visit, you can avoid the higher enforcement fee.

What to Do If Bailiffs Are Coming

Immediate Steps:

  • Don't ignore the situation - it will only get worse
  • Check the notice - ensure you've received proper 7-day notice
  • Contact the bailiff company - try to arrange a payment plan
  • Consider making a offer - based on what you can realistically afford
  • Seek advice - from Citizens Advice or a debt charity

If a Bailiff Visits:

  • You don't have to let them in
  • Speak through a window or use a door chain
  • Ask for identification and the liability order
  • Don't agree to pay more than you can afford
  • Don't sign anything you don't understand

Debt Solutions for Council Tax

Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA)

While council tax can be included in an IVA, it's important to note that arrears from the current year may need special treatment. Benefits include:

  • One affordable monthly payment
  • Interest and charges frozen
  • Legal protection from creditors
  • Remaining debt written off after term ends

Debt Management Plan (DMP)

A less formal arrangement where you make affordable payments to all creditors including the council. Interest may still be added.

Bankruptcy

A last resort that writes off most debts including council tax, but has serious consequences including potential loss of your home.
